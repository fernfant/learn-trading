Lesson 7 · Risk & position sizing — don't blow up

Beginners obsess over what to trade. The truth that keeps you alive is how much you bet. You can have a real winning edge and still go broke by betting too big — a normal run of losses wipes you out first. The fix is one line: risk a small fixed fraction of equity per trade: qty = risk_frac × equity / stop_distance.

💡 Size to the stop, not to a hunch. With $10,000 and a 2% rule you risk $200 a trade. A tight stop $0.50 away lets you hold 400 units; a wide $5.00 stop forces you down to 40 — the dollars at risk stay $200 either way. The stop distance, not your confidence, sets the size: qty = risk_frac × equity / stop_distance.
The drawdown trap. Losses and gains aren't symmetric — recovering a loss needs a bigger gain than the loss itself (gain = loss / (1 − loss)). Big bets dig deep holes, and deep holes demand heroic climbs:
down 10%need +11%
down 20%need +25%
down 50%need +100%
down 75%need +300%
down 90%need +900%
This arithmetic is why capital preservation is rule #1.
✅ You can now say…
  • How much you bet matters more than what you bet — over-betting ruins a winning edge.
  • Fixed-fractional sizing: risk a small fixed fraction (1–2%) of equity per trade.
  • The line you added to market.py: qty = int(risk_frac * equity / stop_distance).
  • A wider stop ⇒ smaller size (same dollar risk); bigger equity ⇒ bigger size.
  • Drawdowns aren't symmetric — down 50% needs +100% back. Risk of ruin is real math, not a scare story.
