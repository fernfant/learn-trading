Lesson 3 · Your first trade — long & short

A price isn't something you just watch — it's something you bet on. On capital.com that bet is a CFD, and the first thing it needs is a direction. Go long and you win when the price rises; go short and you win when it falls.

🎯 Open a trade, then step the market

Pick a side. You open at the bad side of the spread (long buys high, short sells low), and we mark your bet to market each day. The dashed gold line is break-even — a full spread away from your entry, because you'll pay the toll again to close.

position FLAT
Pick a side to open 10 units at mid $100.00.
—
🔁 One formula, both directions: P&L = position × (exit − entry). Long is position = +10, short is −10. When the price drops, the bracket goes negative — and negative × negative is a profit for the short. Watch the grey "other side" line: it's your P&L mirrored.
Shorts cut both ways. A long can only fall to zero, but a price can rise forever — so a short's loss is, in theory, unlimited. And notice both sides open a little red: that's the Lesson 2 spread, paid on the way in and again on the way out. Direction picks which way you need the market to move; the spread is the toll either way.
✅ You can now say…
  • Long = buy first, profit if it rises. Short = sell first, profit if it falls.
  • A whole trade is one signed number: position (+long, −short, 0 flat).
  • You open and close on the bad side of the spread, so a round trip always costs a full spread.
  • The one line you added to market.py: position += 10 (or −= 10 to short).
