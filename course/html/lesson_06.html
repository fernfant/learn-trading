Lesson 6 · Order types — fill on your terms

Every trade so far filled now, at the current price — a market order. But the platform's menu has six entries, each a different answer to when should this fill, and at what price? A limit waits for your price. A stop fires when a level is crossed. A trailing stop follows you up. A take-profit banks a gain. And a GSLO is a stop that can't be slipped by a gap — for a fee.

🎚️ Place your orders, then run the market

You're long 10 units from the entry. Drag each order line with +/− to set it relative to the current price, then hit Run. Watch the day each order fires and the price flash where it filled. Toggle the GSLO on to see what a guaranteed stop does when the market gaps.

💡 A limit gives you price, a market gives you certainty. The limit buy only fills if the price comes to you — a better entry, but it can sit there forever. A market order always fills, but never at a price you chose. Every order on the menu is a point on that trade-off.
A stop promises to fire, not to fill at its price. Press Run with a gap: the market leaps overnight straight through your stop, and a plain stop fills at the next available price — well past where you set it. That extra loss is slippage. Flip the GSLO on and run the gap again: it fills at exactly your level, because you paid a premium for that guarantee. No free lunch — just a choice about who carries the gap risk.
✅ You can now say…
  • Market = fill now, no price control. Limit = your price or better, but may never fill.
  • Stop = becomes a market order once a trigger is crossed (cut losses or enter on a breakout).
  • Trailing stop follows the price up and only ratchets one way, locking in profit.
  • Take-profit auto-banks a gain; a GSLO fills at exactly its trigger even on a gap, for a fee.
  • The line you added to market.py: each day, check triggers — a stop-loss and take-profit that close the position when the mark crosses them.
