Lesson 5 · Leverage & margin — the double-edged sword

Leverage lets you control a big notional position with a small margin deposit: margin = notional / leverage. The broker fronts the rest. That multiplies your gains and your losses against your own cash. capital.com watches your margin level (equity / used margin) and force-closes you at 50% — the stop-out. Crank the slider up and feel how a tiny move blows the account.

⚡ Open a leveraged trade and watch the margin meter

You have $1,000. Pick a leverage, go long or short, then step the market. The MARGIN METER shows your margin level; it drains toward the gold 50% line as equity falls. Hit it and capital.com force-closes you.

💡 Same bet, louder. The dollar move on your position is identical at every leverage — leverage only shrinks the margin you put up. So a fixed loss eats a bigger and bigger fraction of your cash as you crank the slider. At 1x a 2% dip is a scratch; at 20x the same dip erases 40% of your margin.
The stop-out is automatic and merciless. When margin level hits 50%, capital.com closes your position for you — you don't choose the moment, and the loss is locked in. At high leverage you can be in the warning zone after a single ordinary day. This is the number-one way small accounts blow up. Start on the 1:1 (1X) demo.
✅ You can now say…
  • Notional = |position| × price; margin = notional / leverage — the cash the broker locks up.
  • Leverage multiplies gains and losses as a % of your cash; the dollar move on the position is unchanged.
  • Margin level = equity / used_margin. capital.com warns at 75% / 100% and stops you out at 50%.
  • The line you added to market.py: margin = abs(position)*price/leverage, plus the 50% stop-out check.
  • Higher leverage = a smaller move wipes you. Lower leverage = more room to be wrong.
