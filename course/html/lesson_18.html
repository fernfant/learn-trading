Lesson 18 · Be the market maker — quote both sides, earn the spread

The payoff of Track 2. You don't predict direction. You post a bid a little below fair value and an ask a little above, and you wait. A buyer lifts your ask, a seller hits your bid — you bought low, sold high, and pocketed the spread. The very spread you paid as a customer in Track 1, now earned. The catch: it only works while buys and sells balance.

💡 You captured the spread without an opinion. Under balanced flow your P&L climbs at about half a spread per fill — buy at the bid, sell at the ask, repeat. Notice cash swings wildly and even goes negative; ignore it. Only cash + inventory × fair tells the truth, exactly like equity back in Lesson 4.
Inventory is the lurking risk. Hit One-sided flow: every taker buys, so you keep selling, your inventory marches negative (short), and the buying drags fair value up — against you. You earned the spread on every fill, yet your marked P&L bleeds red. Taming that is inventory skew (L19); the informed flow that does it on purpose is adverse selection (L21).
✅ You can now say…
  • A market maker posts bid = fair − h and ask = fair + h and waits — no direction call.
  • A buyer lifts your ask (you sell); a seller hits your bid (you buy) — you always take the opposite side.
  • Over balanced flow you bank ~the spread per round trip; the cost you paid in Track 1 is now revenue.
  • Honest P&L = cash + inventory × fair; cash alone lies.
  • One-sided flow leaves you holding inventory at risk — the wound L19 and L21 treat.
