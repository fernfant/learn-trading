Lesson 1 · What is a price?

Before you can trade against a price, you have to know what a price is. It is not a fixed fact like "this chocolate bar costs $2." It is a living number that wanders — and the single most important, most annoying fact in trading is that you can't know which way it goes next.

📈 Watch a price wander

Every day the price gets nudged by a small random shock — up just as often as down, usually small, occasionally big. Nobody picks the shock. Step it a day at a time, or let it run, and watch a line appear out of pure chance.

Reset a few times. Some markets drift up, some crash, some chop sideways — same rule every time. The shape is an accident.

💡 This is called a random walk. The wiggly line looks like it has trends and patterns you could trade — your brain is wired to see them. The next demo tests whether those patterns are real.

🪙 Beat the coin

The classic beginner instinct: "it went up, so it'll keep going up" (momentum). Predict tomorrow's move. We reveal the real shock and keep score. See if you can stay above 50% — a coin flip.

your hit rate —   (0 right of 0)

Let the robot play the "same as yesterday" strategy 10,000 times. It lands on ~50.0%. In a true random walk, yesterday tells you nothing about today.

Why this matters: real markets aren't perfectly random — but they're close enough that beating a coin flip, after costs, is the entire hard game. Most people who think they see a pattern are losing to randomness and fees. Track 1 is about finding a real edge; Lesson 2 introduces the first cost that eats it — the spread.
✅ You can now say…
  • A price is a number that changes every time someone trades — it wanders.
  • The core model is a random walk: price += shock, a fresh random nudge each step.
  • In a pure random walk, predicting the next move beats a coin flip 0% of the time — it stays at 50%.
