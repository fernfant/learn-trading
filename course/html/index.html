Learn to Trade — from "what's a price?" to high-frequency trading

Two tracks, evenly split. You learn by building: grow one program per track, one line per lesson, until they're the real thing.

🎯 Your mission

A stock price isn't a fact — it's a number that wanders, and nobody knows which way it goes next. Track 1 teaches you to trade against it on a simulator that behaves like a capital.com demo account: two prices, long & short, leverage, the spread, real orders, and the costs that quietly decide who wins.

Track 2 flips the table: you build the exchange and a market-making bot that earns the spread you paid in Track 1 — the very role capital.com plays for its users. That's high-frequency trading, from the inside.

🪙 First, feel the problem: can you beat a coin?

This is a random walk — the simplest honest model of a market. Watch one tick, then predict the next. Tally how often you're right. (Spoiler: about half.)

right 0 / 0  =  —

Hovering near 50%? That's the whole, hard game. Real markets aren't perfectly random — but the edges are small, and telling a real edge from a lucky streak is what this course is about.

🔁 The big idea: the spread, from both seats

The same number ties the two tracks together. As a customer you pay the spread; as a market maker you earn it.

Track 1 · you are the customer

You cross the spread

Buy a hair high, sell a hair low. That gap is your cost on every trade — capital.com's fee.

Track 2 · you are the market maker

You quote the spread
