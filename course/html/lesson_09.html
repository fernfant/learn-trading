Lesson 9 · Costs that kill — spread, swap & slippage

You have a signal now. On paper it makes money. So why does capital.com's own warning say the large majority of retail CFD accounts lose? Because of three quiet costs a "gross" backtest never shows: the spread (paid every round trip), the overnight swap (paid every day held), and slippage (paid on every fill). None is dramatic alone. Stacked, they decide who wins.

The spread is a per-trade toll. Push trades per month up and the curve buckles even with a thin spread — because you pay it on every round trip. Overtrading is the most common way a real edge bleeds out: you pay the toll twenty times to earn it once.
💡 In market.py this is the line cash -= half_spread * abs(position) on every fill, plus cash -= OVERNIGHT_SWAP * abs(position) on every held day, with slippage folded into the fill price. That's why the backtest there reports equity net of costs — the only honest score.
✅ You can now say…
  • Spread is paid in full per round trip (half on open, half on close); frequency multiplies it.
  • Overnight swap is a small daily charge for holding a leveraged position past the cutoff.
  • Slippage makes market orders fill a touch worse than quoted — on every fill.
  • Stacked, these turn many gross-winning strategies into net losers — why most retail CFD accounts lose.
  • An honest backtest scores equity after costs, never before.
