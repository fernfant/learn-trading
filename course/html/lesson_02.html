Lesson 2 · Bid, ask & the spread

Lesson 1 said a stock has a price. It lied. On capital.com there are always two — and you never get the nice one.

🪜 The quote ladder — drag the spread

capital.com quotes a BUY price (you buy high) and a SELL price (you sell low), straddling the fair mid. The gap is the spread. Widen it and watch the cost grow.

📈 The spread is a hurdle

You went long at the BUY price. To exit you must SELL (lower). So the mid has to climb by a full spread before you even break even. Step the market and watch your P&L lag behind the mid.

On capital.com: spreads are tighter on big liquid markets, wider on small/volatile ones, and widen outside market hours. Trade more often → pay the spread more often. It's why a fresh position shows a small red number the instant you open it.
🔁 Foreshadow: someone is on the other side of this spread, collecting it. In Track 2 that someone is you — the market maker.
