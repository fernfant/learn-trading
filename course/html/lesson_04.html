Lesson 4 · P&L & equity — what are you worth?

You can open a trade. Now: am I up or down? Two numbers answer it. Balance is your settled cash — it sits still while a trade is open. Equity is your live net worth — cash plus the value of what you're holding. The gap between them is your unrealized P&L: paper, not money, until you close.

💡 Equity is the honest scoreboard. The instant you open, equity drops by a full spread — it already counts the toll you'd pay to exit (Lesson 2). Balance won't show that loss until you actually close. Watch the curve start below the dashed $2000 line.
Paper isn't cash. A juicy unrealized profit can evaporate before you close — it was never in your balance. Many traders blow up by treating equity like money in the bank. On capital.com, every margin warning in the next lesson is driven by equity, not balance.
✅ You can now say…
  • Balance = settled cash (moves only on close). Equity = cash + live position value.
  • Unrealized P&L is paper and moves every tick; realized P&L is locked into cash when you close.
  • The line you added to market.py: equity = cash + position * price.
  • Equity already reflects the spread you'd pay to exit — it's what really matters.
