Lesson 22 · 🏆 Capstone — profitable and flat

The grand finale of Track 2. Combine everything: quote both sides to earn the spread (L18), skew by inventory to stay flat (L19), and survive a flow that hides toxic, informed traders (L21). A real market maker has a dual objective — end profitable (positive P&L from captured spread) and flat (inventory near zero, not a giant directional bet). Either one alone is easy and worthless. Both at once is the job.

💡 Read the decomposition, not just the headline. Total P&L splits into three pieces that sum exactly: spread earned (your edge), adverse loss (toxic ticks moving against your inventory, L21), and inventory drift (fair's wander on whatever you're holding — pure risk). A maker whose P&L is mostly drift isn't making a market, it's gambling on leftover inventory.
The honest tension. Widen the spread and skew harder to survive adverse selection — but go too far and your quotes sit so far from fair that nobody hits you: flat, safe, and earning nothing. Quote too tight and toxic flow eats the thin spread. Push informed flow high enough and no setting stays profitable — the flow is simply poison. There is no free money on this side of the book either.
✅ You can now say…
  • A real market maker's objective is dual: end profitable (positive P&L from captured spread) and flat (inventory near zero).
  • Either half alone is trivial and worthless — profit alone is a lucky directional bet; flat alone means you quoted so wide you never traded.
  • The skew (L19) keeps you flat without changing who trades — it changes your prices so the market hands your inventory back.
  • Total P&L = spread earned + adverse-selection loss + inventory drift; the edge is the spread, eroded from both sides.
  • The edge is thin and it dies if toxic flow is too high or the spread too tight — no free money, the same honest lesson as Track 1.
